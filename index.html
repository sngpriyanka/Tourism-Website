Why Nepal?

A Land Where Gods Still Walk Among Us

Nepal — birthplace of Lord Buddha, home to the mighty Everest, and a living museum of ancient cultures. Every cobblestone in Kathmandu, every prayer flag in the mountains, and every smile from the locals tells a story thousands of years old.

With 8 of the world's 14 highest peaks, over 6,000 rivers, dense subtropical jungles hiding Bengal tigers, and medieval city squares frozen in time — Nepal offers more diversity per square mile than anywhere else on Earth.
